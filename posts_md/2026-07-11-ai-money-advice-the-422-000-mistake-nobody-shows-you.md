## Background

Let me introduce you to Maya. She's 27, makes $61,000 a year, and just crossed $5,000 in savings for the first time in her life. Huge moment. So she does what most of us would do in 2026 — she opens ChatGPT and types: 'I have $5,000 saved. What should I do with it?' The answer sounds smart. Polished. Confident. It tells her to keep her money 'safe and liquid' in a savings account, mentions an interest rate that hasn't been accurate in two years, and — here's the killer — never once asks if her employer offers a 401(k) match. Hers does. Fifty cents on the dollar, up to 6%. That's a guaranteed 50% return she's leaving on the table, and the AI never brought it up because it doesn't know she exists. Maya follows the advice. Feels responsible. Feels done. And that one conversation — five minutes, zero pushback — quietly sets her on a path that costs her more than the price of a house.
