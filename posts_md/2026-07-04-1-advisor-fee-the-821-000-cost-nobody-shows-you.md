## Background

Meet Maya. She's 27, works in marketing, makes $68,000 a year, and just crossed $30,000 in savings — her first real money milestone. Her parents are proud, and they do what parents do: they send her to their financial advisor. Nice office, firm handshake, and a pitch that sounds completely reasonable: 'We charge just 1% a year. That's it. No commissions, no surprises.' One percent. Maya thinks: on my $30K, that's $300 a year. Less than my phone bill. She signs. Here's what nobody in that office says out loud: the fee isn't $300 once. It's 1% of everything, every year, forever — including all the growth her money would have earned. And this week, the Financial Times reported that the head of a major wealth management industry body warned that firms use — his words — 'deliberate obfuscation' around fees. Statements that show percentages instead of dollars. Fees deducted quietly from returns so you never write a check and never feel the cost. Maya will never get a bill for $821,000. She'll just... never have it. Today we're going to make that invisible fee visible, with real numbers.
